<p><p>Artificial wood-based board, also known as engineered wood or composite wood, is a type of wood product manufactured by binding or fixing the strands, fibers, particles, or veneers of wood together with adhesives. They offer several advantages including cost-effectiveness, improved dimensional stability, resistance to moisture, and enhanced strength and durability.</p><p>The artificial wood-based board market is witnessing significant growth due to the increasing demand for green construction materials, rising disposable income, and rapid urbanization. <p><p>The artificial wood-based board market is segmented into various types, including particle board, plywood, oriented strand board (OSB), medium density fiberboard (MDF)/high density fiberboard (HDF), hardboard, and others. Particle board is made from small wood particles bonded together with resin, while plywood consists of multiple layers of thin wood veneers glued together. OSB is engineered wood made from strands of wood that are layered and bonded with resin. MDF/HDF is produced from fine wood fibers and offers better strength and smoothness. Hardboard is a dense and durable composite board made from compressed wood fibers.</p></p>
