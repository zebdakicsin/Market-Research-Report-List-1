<p><p>Bioabsorbable bone fixation screws are medical devices that are used to hold bones together during the healing process. Unlike traditional metal screws, these screws are made from bioabsorbable materials that gradually break down and get absorbed by the body over time, eliminating the need for a second surgery to remove them.</p><p>The future outlook for the bioabsorbable bone fixation screws market is promising. The increasing prevalence of orthopedic injuries and disorders, along with the growing preference for minimally invasive surgeries, is driving the demand for these screws. Moreover, advancements in material science and manufacturing techniques are leading to the development of more effective and biocompatible bioabsorbable materials, further boosting the market growth.</p><p>In addition, the aging population and rising healthcare expenditure are contributing to the market's growth. As the elderly are more prone to fractures and mobility disorders, there is a greater need for innovative bone fixation solutions. Furthermore, the integration of bioabsorbable screws with other orthopedic implants, such as plates and rods, is expected to drive the market further.</p><p>However, challenges such as high costs associated with bioabsorbable implants and the lack of reimbursement policies in certain regions may hinder the market's growth.
