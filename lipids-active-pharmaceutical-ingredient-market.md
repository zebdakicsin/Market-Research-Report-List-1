Simple lipids, such as fatty acids and triglycerides, are basic lipid structures found in naturally occurring fats and oils. Compound lipids, such as phospholipids and glycolipids, contain additional components like phosphoric acid or carbohydrates. Derived lipids, including steroids and terpenes, are synthesized from simple or compound lipids. Each lipid class has distinct pharmaceutical applications due to their unique structures and properties, making them valuable ingredients in various medications and therapies.</p></p>
