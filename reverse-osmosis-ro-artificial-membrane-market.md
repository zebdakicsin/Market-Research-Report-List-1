<p><p>Reverse Osmosis (RO) is a process that uses an artificial membrane to remove impurities from water. There are two main types of RO membranes in the market: cellulose-based membrane and thin film composite membrane. Cellulose-based membranes are made from natural cellulose materials and are generally less expensive, but have limitations in durability and efficiency. Thin film composite membranes, on the other hand, are composed of multiple layers and offer better performance in terms of efficiency, durability, and resistance to fouling.</p></p>
