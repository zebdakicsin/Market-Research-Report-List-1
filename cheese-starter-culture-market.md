<p><p>Cheese starter cultures are significant in the cheese-making process as they are responsible for initiating fermentation and influencing the final taste, texture, and aroma of the cheese. There are two main types of cheese starter cultures: mesophilic and thermophilic. Mesophilic cultures are predominantly used in the production of soft and semi-soft cheeses due to their ability to thrive at lower temperatures. In contrast, thermophilic cultures are used for the production of harder cheeses and can withstand higher temperatures. Both types play a vital role in determining the characteristics and quality of different types of cheeses.</p></p>
