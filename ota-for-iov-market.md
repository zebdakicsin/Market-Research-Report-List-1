<p><p>OTA, or Over-the-Air, refers to the ability to update software and firmware remotely without the need for physical intervention. In the Internet of Vehicles (IoV) market, there are two main types of OTA updates: FOTA (Firmware Over-the-Air) and SOTA (Software Over-the-Air). FOTA allows for the remote updates of a device's firmware, which controls its basic functions. SOTA, on the other hand, enables the remote updates of a device's software, including applications and interfaces. Both FOTA and SOTA are essential for keeping vehicles in the IoV market up-to-date, secure, and functioning optimally.</p></p>
