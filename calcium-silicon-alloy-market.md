<p><p>Calcium Silicon Alloy is primarily divided into two market types: High Purity Type and General Type. High Purity Type refers to an alloy with a high concentration of calcium and silicon, usually above 95%. It is mainly used in the production of special alloys with strict purity requirements, such as stainless steel. On the other hand, General Type refers to an alloy with a lower concentration of calcium and silicon, usually ranging from 60% to 90%. It is commonly used in the manufacturing of cast iron, ductile iron, and other general alloy products.</p></p>
