<p><p>Ethylene Oxide (EO) is produced through different processes in the market, including SD-Oxidation, Shell-Oxidation, Dow-Oxidation, and Others. SD-Oxidation refers to the direct oxidation of ethylene using silver catalysts. Shell-Oxidation involves the oxidation of ethylene using a silver-zeolite catalyst. Dow-Oxidation refers to the catalytic oxidation of ethylene using a mixed metal oxide catalyst. Lastly, the "Others" category encompasses alternative or proprietary processes employed by different manufacturers. These various methods allow for the production of ethylene oxide, which is widely used in industries such as plastics, textiles, and detergents.</p></p>
