<p><p>The market for automotive exhaust gas recirculation (EGR) can be classified into two types: pneumatic EGR valve and electric EGR valve. Pneumatic EGR valves are operated using air pressure and are commonly found in older vehicle models. Electric EGR valves, on the other hand, are controlled electronically and are prevalent in modern vehicles. Both types function to reduce nitrogen oxide emissions by recirculating a portion of exhaust gas back into the engine. However, the difference lies in the method of operation, with pneumatic valves relying on air pressure and electric valves utilizing electronic control.</p></p>
