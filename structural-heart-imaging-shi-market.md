<p><p>Structural Heart Imaging (SHI) encompasses various types of medical imaging techniques used to assess the structure and function of the heart. Echocardiogram refers to a non-invasive imaging technique that uses ultrasound waves to create real-time images of the heart's chambers, valves, and blood flow. Angiogram, on the other hand, typically refers to an invasive imaging technique, such as cardiac catheterization, which involves injecting a contrast dye into the blood vessels to visualize any blockages or abnormalities. Both echocardiogram and angiogram play crucial roles in diagnosing and monitoring heart conditions.</p></p>
