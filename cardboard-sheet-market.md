<p><p>The cardboard sheet market can be divided into two main types: Native Paper and Recycling Paper. Native Paper refers to cardboard sheets that are made from virgin fibers obtained directly from trees, without any recycled content. These sheets are considered to be of higher quality and are often used for products that require strength or durability. On the other hand, Recycling Paper cardboard sheets are made from recycled paper fibers, which are processed and reformed into sheets. These sheets are more environmentally friendly and are used in various industries for packaging and other non-structural applications.</p></p>
