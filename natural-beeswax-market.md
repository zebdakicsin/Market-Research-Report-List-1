<p><p>The natural beeswax market consists of different types, including yellow beeswax and white beeswax. Yellow beeswax is unbleached and retains its natural color, ranging from pale yellow to dark amber. It is commonly used in cosmetic products, candles, and pharmaceuticals. On the other hand, white beeswax undergoes a bleaching process, resulting in a white or light yellow appearance. It is often preferred in the food and cosmetics industries, as it provides a neutral color and odorless properties. Both types of beeswax serve various purposes, depending on their specific characteristics and applications.</p></p>
