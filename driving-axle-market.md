<p><p>The driving axle market consists of two main types: non-disconnected and disconnected axles. A non-disconnected axle, also known as a live axle, transmits power to both wheels at all times. This type is commonly used in rear-wheel drive vehicles and provides better traction and stability. In contrast, a disconnected axle, also known as an independent axle, allows power to be distributed independently to each wheel. This type is commonly used in front-wheel drive vehicles and provides better maneuverability and fuel efficiency.</p></p>
