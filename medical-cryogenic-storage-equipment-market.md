<p><p>Medical cryogenic storage equipment is used to store and preserve biological samples, blood, vaccines, and medications at specific temperatures. Blood refrigerators maintain a temperature of 4℃ with a ±1℃ fluctuation. Medical refrigerators keep a temperature between 2℃ and 8℃, suitable for storing vaccines and temperature-sensitive medications. Biomedical freezers have a temperature range of -10℃ to -50℃ and are used for long-term storage of biological samples. Ultra low freezers operate at temperatures ranging from -50℃ to -150℃, commonly used for storing cell cultures and laboratory materials. Liquid nitrogen tanks are used to store samples at extremely low temperatures between -150℃ and -196℃. Additionally, there are other types of cryogenic storage equipment available in the market for various specific applications.</p></p>
