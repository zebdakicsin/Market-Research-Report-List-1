<p><p>An I-joist, also known as a wooden I-beam, is a structural component commonly used in residential and commercial construction. It is made up of two flanges, or chords, made of structural composite lumber (SCL), either laminated veneer lumber (LVL) or oriented strand board (OSB), and a webbing material that connects them. I-joists offer several advantages over traditional solid wood joists, including longer spans, increased strength and stiffness, reduced waste, and ease of installation.</p><p>The global I-joist market has been experiencing significant growth and is expected to continue to expand at a CAGR of 11% over the forecast period.
