<p><p>Bridge movement joints are crucial components in bridge construction that allow for necessary movement and expansion, thus ensuring the structural integrity of the bridge. There are two types of bridge movement joints: open joints and closed joints. Open joints are designed to accommodate larger movements and are typically used in bridges with long spans or high traffic loads. Closed joints, on the other hand, restrict movements to a certain range and are commonly used in bridges with shorter spans or lower traffic loads. Both types play a vital role in enhancing the durability and safety of bridge structures.</p></p>
