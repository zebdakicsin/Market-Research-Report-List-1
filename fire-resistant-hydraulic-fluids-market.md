<p><p>Fire-resistant hydraulic fluids are used to reduce the risk of fire accidents in hydraulic systems. There are four main types of fire-resistant hydraulic fluids: HFA, HFB, HFC, and HFD. HFA fluids contain water, which evaporates during operation, creating a steam layer that acts as a fire barrier. HFB fluids are water-in-oil emulsions, providing more fire resistance than HFA. HFC fluids are water glycols, with high water content for increased fire resistance. HFD fluids, such as synthetic esters, offer the highest level of fire resistance due to their non-combustible properties.</p></p>
