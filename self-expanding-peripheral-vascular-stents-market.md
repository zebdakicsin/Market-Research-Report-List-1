<p><p>Self-expanding peripheral vascular stents refer to medical devices that are used to treat blocked or narrowed blood vessels in the peripheral vasculature. These stents come in different types, with two common ones being Elgiloy and Nitinol stents. Elgiloy stents are made from a high-performance alloy called Elgiloy, which offers excellent radiopacity and flexibility. On the other hand, Nitinol stents are made from a shape memory alloy called Nitinol, which can adapt to different vessel sizes and provide sustained radial strength. Both types are widely used in treating peripheral vascular diseases.</p></p>
