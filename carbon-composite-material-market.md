<p><p>The carbon composite material market encompasses various types of manufacturing processes. Chemical Vapor Deposition involves the deposition of carbon atoms on a substrate by exposing it to hydrocarbon gases in a high-temperature environment. The Liquid Impregnation Process involves the infiltration of a carbonizable resin into a fibrous preform, followed by carbonization. Both processes are used to produce carbon composite materials, which possess high strength, low weight, and excellent thermal and electrical conductivity, making them desirable for various applications in industries such as aerospace, automotive, and electronics.</p></p>
