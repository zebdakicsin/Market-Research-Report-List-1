<p><p>The commercial vehicle steel wheel market can be categorized into three types: casting, forging, and other. </p><p>Casting involves the process of pouring molten steel into a mold to create the wheel shape. This method is known for its cost-effectiveness and ability to produce complex designs. </p><p>Forging, on the other hand, involves shaping the steel wheel by applying pressure and heat to it. This technique results in a strong and durable product, suitable for heavy-duty commercial vehicles. </p><p>The "other" category encompasses alternative manufacturing methods, such as rim spinning or sheet metal forming, which may offer unique advantages or cater to specific requirements.</p></p>
