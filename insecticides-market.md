<p><p>The insecticides market is diverse and can be categorized into five main types: pyrethroids, organophosphorus, carbamates, organochlorine, and botanicals. Pyrethroids are synthetic insecticides that mimic natural pyrethrins, while organophosphorus compounds are nerve-poisons that disrupt insect nervous systems. Carbamates also act on insect nerves but are less toxic to mammals. Organochlorine insecticides, such as DDT, persist in the environment and have been largely banned due to their health and ecological risks. Lastly, botanical insecticides are derived from plants and are considered more environmentally friendly alternatives to synthetic insecticides.</p></p>
