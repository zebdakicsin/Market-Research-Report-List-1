<p><p>DNA modifying enzymes refer to a class of proteins that can add or remove genetic material in DNA molecules. Alkaline phosphatases are enzymes that can remove phosphate groups from DNA molecules. Exonucleases can remove nucleotides from the ends of DNA molecules. Endonucleases are enzymes that can cleave DNA molecules at specific internal sites. Terminal transferases can add nucleotides to the ends of DNA molecules. These enzymes are vital for various molecular biology techniques such as DNA sequencing, cloning, and gene editing.</p></p>
