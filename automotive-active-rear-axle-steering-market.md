<p><p>Automotive Active Rear Axle Steering refers to a technology that enables the rear wheels of a vehicle to turn, improving maneuverability and stability. There are two types of systems in the market: single actuator and dual actuator. In a single actuator system, a single motor controls both rear wheels simultaneously, providing basic steering adjustments. On the other hand, a dual actuator system uses two motors, allowing independent control of each rear wheel, thus providing enhanced agility and precision in steering maneuvers.</p></p>
