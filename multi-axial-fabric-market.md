<p><p>Multi-axial fabric refers to a type of material used in various industries for its exceptional strength and versatility. Uniaxial fabric primarily has fibers aligned in a single direction, providing superior tensile strength. Biaxial fabric contains fibers aligned in two directions, enhancing its flexibility and resistance to warping. Triaxial fabric involves fibers aligned in three directions, providing excellent support in different axes. Quadaxial fabric consists of four-directional fibers, offering increased strength and stability. Other multi-axial fabrics may include fabrics with varying arrangements of fibers to suit specific requirements, such as bi-directional or helical orientations.</p></p>
