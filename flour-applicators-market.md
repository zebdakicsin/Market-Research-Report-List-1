<p><p>Flour applicators are used in the food industry to evenly distribute flour onto various products. There are two main types of flour applicators: automatic and semi-automatic. </p><p>Automatic flour applicators are fully mechanized and require minimal human intervention. They are equipped with advanced features like sensors and programmable settings, allowing for precise flour distribution. These applicators are typically used in large-scale industrial settings where high production volume and consistent quality are essential.</p><p>Semi-automatic flour applicators, on the other hand, require some manual intervention. They usually involve a combination of manual flour placement and automatic flour spreading mechanisms. These applicators are commonly used in smaller-scale food production facilities, bakeries, and kitchens where moderate production volumes are expected, and a balance between automation and human control is preferred.</p></p>
