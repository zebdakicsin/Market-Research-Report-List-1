<p><p>The sulfur-containing silane market is comprised of two main types: polysulfide type and sulfhydryl type. Polysulfide type silanes contain multiple sulfur atoms in their structure, making them highly reactive and capable of forming strong bonds with various materials. These silanes are commonly used as adhesion promoters and sealants in industries such as construction and automotive. On the other hand, sulfhydryl type silanes contain one sulfur atom and are known for their strong adhesive properties and ability to act as coupling agents in the production of rubber and plastics. Both types play a crucial role in enhancing the performance and durability of various products.</p></p>
