<p><p>The dried flowers market offers various types of drying methods for flowers, including air-drying, press-drying, embedded-drying, oven-drying, glycerine-drying, and freeze-drying. Air-drying involves hanging flowers upside down in a well-ventilated area, while press-drying involves placing them between heavy books. Embedded-drying requires the use of silica gel, which absorbs moisture from the flowers. Oven-drying involves baking flowers at a low temperature, while glycerine-drying involves soaking them in a mixture of glycerine and water. Freeze-drying preserves the flowers' natural shape and color by freezing them and removing moisture through sublimation.</p></p>
