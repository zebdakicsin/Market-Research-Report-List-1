<p><p>Roof distributed photovoltaic power stations are categorized into two market types: crystalline silicon photovoltaic power station and thin film photovoltaic power station. Crystalline silicon photovoltaic power stations use solar cells made of crystalline silicon to convert sunlight into electricity. These solar cells are more efficient but costlier to manufacture. On the other hand, thin film photovoltaic power stations use solar cells that are made by depositing thin layers of semiconducting materials onto a substrate. Although less efficient, these solar cells are cheaper to produce and can be integrated into various surfaces.</p></p>
