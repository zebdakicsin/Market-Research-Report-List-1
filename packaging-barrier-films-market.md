<p><p>Packaging barrier films serve as a protective layer for food and other perishable items by preventing oxygen, moisture, and other external factors from entering the package. Different types of barrier films include metallized films, which have a thin layer of metal for enhanced barrier properties; inorganic oxide coated films with a layer of inorganic materials like silicon oxide; organic coated/laminated films, which have a layer of organic coatings for added protection; and coextruded films, which are made by combining multiple materials to create a barrier against external elements.</p></p>
