<p><ul><li>X-ray Based</li><li>Gamma-ray Based</li><li>Proton-beam Based</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Radiology surgical robots refer to sophisticated systems utilized in surgical procedures to enhance precision and accuracy. These robots are categorized into three types based on the technology employed: X-ray based, gamma-ray based, and proton-beam based. X-ray based robots employ X-ray imaging technology to provide real-time images, aiding surgeons in navigating through tissues during surgeries. Gamma-ray based robots utilize gamma-ray emitters to precisely locate anatomical structures, facilitating surgical interventions. Proton-beam based robots utilize high-energy proton beams to treat tumors with greater precision, minimizing damage to surrounding healthy tissues.</p></p>
