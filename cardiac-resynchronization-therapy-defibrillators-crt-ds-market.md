<p><p>Cardiac Resynchronization Therapy Defibrillators (CRT-Ds) are medical devices used to treat heart failure and certain heart rhythm disorders. They combine the functions of an implantable cardioverter defibrillator (ICD) and a cardiac resynchronization therapy (CRT) device. CRT-Ds are surgically implanted in the chest and deliver electrical impulses to help synchronize the heart's pumping action. They can also provide electric shocks to restore normal heart rhythm in case of a life-threatening arrhythmia. On the other hand, external defibrillators (ED) are portable devices used outside of the body for delivering electric shocks to restore normal heart rhythm in emergency situations.</p></p>
